English

Amazing grace! how sweet the sound,
That saved a wretch; like me!
I once was lost, but now am found,
Was blind, but now I see.

’Twas grace that taught my heart to fear,
And grace my fears relieved;
How precious did that grace appear
The hour I first believed!

The Lord hath promised good to me,
His word my hope secures;
He will my shield and portion be
As long as life endures.

When we’ve been there ten thousand years,
Bright shining as the sun,
We’ve no less days to sing God’s praise
Than when we first begun.

Twi

Adom anwonwade! ɛte sɛn nneɛma no,
A gyee ɔbɔnefo; te sɛ me!
Me yerae dada, nanso seesei wɔahu me,
Na mennhu, nanso seesei mahu.

Na adom na kyerɛɛ me koma sɛ mennsuro,
Na adom nso yi me suro kɔe;
Ɛyɛɛ anika sɛnea saa adom no baa
Bere a megyinaa gyidi mu kan no!

Awurade hyɛɛ me bɔ pa,
Ne nsɛm na m'ankasa anidaso so da hɔ;
Ɔbɛyɛ me kyɛm ne me kyɛfa
Ɛkɔsi sɛ nkwa to ntwa me so.

Sɛ yɛwɔ hɔ mfe du mpem,
A yɛhyerɛn te sɛ owia,
Yɛn nna nnwɔtwe nso mfi yɛn nsa so sɛ yɛbɛto Onyankopɔn ayeyi
Sɛnea ɛyɛe bere a yɛde yɛn ho hyɛɛ ase no.
